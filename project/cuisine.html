Ghanaian Cuisine

Ghanaian cuisine is known for its bold flavors, rich spices, and deep cultural roots. Many traditional dishes are shared during festivals, family gatherings, and community celebrations. Below are some of the most beloved meals from across Ghana.

Jollof Rice

Jollof is a popular West African rice dish enjoyed across Ghana. Prepared with tomatoes, peppers, onions, and spices, it is often served with chicken, beef, or fish. Ghanaian Jollof is widely celebrated for its unique taste and vibrant color.

Waakye

Waakye is a traditional rice-and-beans dish cooked with sorghum leaves to achieve its distinct brown color. It is usually served with stew, boiled eggs, gari, spaghetti, plantains, and fish or meat. It is a popular breakfast and lunch option in Ghana.

Banku with Grilled Tilapia

Banku is prepared from fermented corn and cassava dough, giving it a slightly tangy flavor. It is commonly paired with grilled tilapia and spicy pepper sauce (shito or fresh pepper). This dish is a staple among the Ga and Ewe communities.
